Comparative Maps

What is a comparative map?

A comparative (or off-backbone) map is a map that is loaded after you have already chosen a backbone map. Because of this the chromosomes that will be loaded are determined by the regions of syteny as loaded into our database. These regions of synteny will appear in the column for the comparative map, color coded by chromosome, along with their start and stop positions to the right of the chromosome segments. Any annotation that exists inside of these regions (in whole or in part) will appear to the left of the segments.

These regions of synteny are determined by the "Net - Level 1" conservation track at UCSC.

You may notice that loading a comparative map takes longer than loading a backbone map. This is because for every syntenic region on the backbone chromosome, we must load a new chromosome for the off-backbone map. Due to the imprecise nature of the genetics of recombination and evolution, their is often a large number of small off-backbone segments that must be loaded.
